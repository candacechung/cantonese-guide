📝 Grammar Playground

Learn how to say "Yes," "No," "Good," and "Bad"!

The "Is" vs "Is Not"

To Be / Yes 係 Haih

Use this to say what something IS.

Not To Be / No 唔係 Mh haih

Use this to say what something IS NOT.

The "Good" vs "Not Good"

Good / Well 好 Hou

Use this for "Good" or "Very."

Not Good / Bad 唔好 Mh hou

Use this for "Not Good" or "Don't."

🚀 Sentence Builder Challenge:

Combine these with your food words!

  • ✅ 好食 (Hou sek) = Good to eat (Delicious!)
  • ❌ 唔好食 (Mh hou sek) = Not good to eat (Yucky!)
  • ✅ 係魚 (Haih yú) = It is a fish.
  • ❌ 唔係魚 (Mh haih yú) = It is not a fish.

The "Have" vs "Not Have" (Existence)

To Have / There is 有 Yáuh

Use this for things you OWN or that EXIST.

To Not Have / There is not 無 Mòuh

Use this when something is MISSING.

💡 Pro Tip: Existence Questions

Just like "好唔好", you can combine these to ask "Do you have?":

「有無...?」 (Yáuh mòuh...?) = "Have or not have...?"

  • ✅ 我有魚 (Ngóh yáuh yú) = I have fish.
  • ❌ 我無錢 (Ngóh mòuh chín) = I have no money.
  • ❓ 有無叉燒包? (Yáuh mòuh chaā sīu baāu?) = Do you have BBQ pork buns?

💡 Pro Tip: The "Faan" (返) Rule

In Cantonese, we don't just "go" to work or home. We use the word 返 (Fāan), which means "to return." Whether it's your office or your house, you are "returning" to where you belong!

Example: Ngóh fāan uk kéi = I am going (returning) home.
